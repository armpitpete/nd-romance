# Quiet Hours — Arc Map Example

## Purpose

This is a filled example using the Arc Map blank.

It shows how the Quiet Hours sample moves from structure into relationship progression.

---

## Project

**Working title:** Quiet Hours

**Primary pairing:** Mara / Ellis

---

# Relationship arc

## Starting state

**Person A starts as:** Mara protects herself through silence, routine, and minimal live emotional exposure.

**Person B starts as:** Ellis protects himself through usefulness, repair, and too much explanation when he feels unsure.

**Relationship starts as:** strangers with indirect familiarity inside the same community building.

---

## First change

**What first changes between them?** Ellis gives Mara written clarity about the lamp instead of forcing a longer spoken exchange.

**Why does it matter?** Mara experiences his attention as careful rather than invasive. Ellis sees that low-pressure communication can be care.

---

## Pattern formation

**What repeated contact or pattern forms?** They begin using short notes between the library desk and the repair desk.

**What becomes easier?** Mara can communicate without being rushed. Ellis can be useful without immediately over-talking.

**What becomes harder?** The notes start to matter emotionally, so each small exchange carries more risk.

---

## Trust opening

**What trust becomes possible?** Mara tells Ellis why the lamp matters. Ellis receives the truth quietly instead of filling the silence.

**What care becomes visible?** Ellis remembers Mara's communication preferences. Mara starts trusting that Ellis can notice without taking over.

---

## Pressure stage

**What pressure complicates the bond?** A noisy public event takes over the community building and disrupts the quiet spaces that make their connection possible.

**What does each person misread?** Ellis reads Mara's withdrawal as rejection. Mara reads Ellis's urgent public help as pressure.

---

## Major turn

**What changes the relationship state?** During the event, Ellis tries to help Mara visibly. Mara shuts down and leaves.

**What is now newly possible or newly difficult?** The relationship can no longer stay safely inside small notes. They must clarify what silence, help, leaving, and care mean between them.

---

## Repair / recognition

**What is understood more accurately?** Mara's leaving was regulation, not rejection. Ellis's over-helping was fear, not control.

**What changes in behaviour?** Mara uses clearer pause signals when she can. Ellis offers one clear option and then waits.

---

## Ending state

**Person A ends as:** Mara can ask for time without disappearing completely.

**Person B ends as:** Ellis can care without solving, chasing, or explaining too much.

**Relationship ends as:** a chosen shared pattern with practical signals, quiet repair, and a future shape that fits both of them.

# Arc verdict

The arc is clear enough to draft from.

The relationship movement is:

1. careful contact
2. written rhythm
3. specific trust
4. care becoming risky
5. public pressure exposing the fault line
6. repair through clearer signals and changed behaviour
7. ending proof through a usable shared pattern

## Main strength

The arc does not depend only on attraction. It tracks communication, sensory pressure, misread care, and changed behaviour.

## Main risk

The middle could become too quiet if the event pressure is introduced too late.

## Recommended adjustment

Seed the public event from chapter 4 or 5 so the outside pressure grows before chapter 7.
