# Quiet Hours — First Use Test

## Purpose

This is a small test project for the ND Romance pack.

It tests whether `FIRST_USE.md` gives enough structure to begin without reading the whole pack first.

---

# 1. Simple romance definition

This is a quiet contemporary romance about two neurodivergent adults who both want closeness but manage safety in opposite ways.

One protects themself through silence and routine.
The other protects themself through usefulness and over-explaining.

The romance works when they stop treating the other person's regulation style as rejection or pressure.

---

# 2. Central pair

## Mara

Mara is a library assistant who prefers predictable shifts, quiet rooms, and written notes over live emotional confrontation.

She is observant, precise, and kind, but she becomes silent when she feels watched or emotionally rushed.

## Ellis

Ellis runs a small repair desk inside the same community building.

He is practical, talkative under stress, and good at fixing objects, but he tries to fix feelings too quickly when he is afraid of being unwanted.

---

# 3. Why these two matter

- Mara notices when Ellis is helping too much because he is anxious, not because he is controlling.
- Ellis notices that Mara's silence is not emptiness; it is processing.
- Both are careful with broken things.
- Both understand that small practical changes can mean love.
- Each offers the other a different kind of safety.

---

# 4. Emotional promise

The story promises that love can become clearer when two people learn the difference between silence, pressure, care, and fear.

---

# 5. Main obstacle

Mara experiences Ellis's urgent helpfulness as pressure.

Ellis experiences Mara's silence as withdrawal.

Neither is trying to hurt the other, but both keep misreading the other's survival strategy.

---

# 6. ND truth layer

The romance is built around:

- delayed processing
- over-explaining under stress
- sensory need for quiet spaces
- care through practical adjustments
- repair after misattunement
- direct clarification instead of social guessing

---

# 7. Ending requirement

The ending only works if Mara can ask for time without disappearing, and Ellis can offer care without trying to solve everything immediately.

The relationship should end with a new shared pattern, not only a confession.

---

# 8. Rough structure path

## Opening contact

Mara brings a broken desk lamp to Ellis's repair desk during the building's quiet hour.

## Deepening bond

They begin leaving short notes with repair requests, reading recommendations, and small practical observations.

## Midpoint change

Ellis notices Mara has changed her lunch break to avoid the loud staff room. He quietly fixes a small unused side room so she has somewhere calm to go.

## Pressure and fault line

Mara feels seen, but also exposed. Ellis worries he has overstepped and starts talking too much to justify himself.

## Rupture or major test

During a noisy community event, Ellis tries to help Mara in public. Mara shuts down and leaves. Ellis reads this as rejection.

## Repair path

Mara writes a clear note explaining that leaving was regulation, not rejection. Ellis replies with one short note instead of a long defence.

## Ending proof

They agree on a shared signal for pause, help, and no-help-yet. The final scene shows them using it naturally during another busy day.

# 9. Planned first scene

## Scene identity

**Scene title:** The Broken Lamp  
**Chapter / position:** Opening scene  
**Point of view:** Mara  
**Location:** Community building repair desk  
**Relationship stage at start:** strangers with indirect familiarity  

## Scene job

First noticing.

## Start state

### Mara

**What does she want?**  
To get the lamp repaired without a long conversation.

**What is she afraid of?**  
Being pulled into live social performance when she is already low on capacity.

**What does she think is happening?**  
A practical errand.

**What is she hiding?**  
That the lamp matters because it belonged to her grandmother.

### Ellis

**What does he want?**  
To repair the lamp properly and not make her uncomfortable.

**What is he afraid of?**  
Getting the interaction wrong and seeming useless or intrusive.

**What does he think is happening?**  
A careful person has trusted him with something important.

**What is he hiding?**  
That he noticed months ago that Mara avoids the loudest spaces.

## Scene conditions

**Sensory load:** low, but fluorescent light hums.  
**Privacy / crowding:** mostly private.  
**Time pressure:** Mara is between shifts.  
**Person A condition:** guarded but regulated.  
**Person B condition:** focused, slightly anxious.  

## Bond evidence

Mara notices that Ellis asks whether she wants a written collection time instead of making her wait for a verbal explanation.

Ellis notices that Mara relaxes slightly when he writes the estimate down.

## Misread / accuracy map

**What Mara thinks is happening:** Ellis is unusually careful for a repair-desk interaction.  
**What Ellis thinks is happening:** Mara wants minimal speech.  
**What is actually happening:** both are creating the first small piece of safe communication.  

## Scene-end change

By the end of the scene, Mara trusts Ellis with the lamp and Ellis understands that written clarity helps her.

## Keep / revise / cut

Keep.

This scene must stay because it creates the first quiet communication pattern.

---

# 10. Test verdict

The first-use workflow worked.

It helped because it reduced the pack to four starting files and defined a small stop point.

Still slightly heavy:

- the design sheet is useful but large
- the structure template could intimidate a new user if they try to fill every section

Recommended future improvement:

Add a one-page mini design sheet for first-pass use only.
