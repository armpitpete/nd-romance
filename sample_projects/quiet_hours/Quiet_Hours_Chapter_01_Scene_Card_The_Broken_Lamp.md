# Quiet Hours — Chapter 1 Scene Card

## Scene title

The Broken Lamp

---

## 1. Scene identity

**Project:** Quiet Hours

**Scene title:** The Broken Lamp

**Chapter / position:** Chapter 1, opening scene

**Point of view:** Mara

**Location:** Ellis's repair desk inside the public community building

**Relationship stage at scene start:** strangers with indirect familiarity

---

## 2. Scene job

**Primary scene job:** first noticing

**Scene job in one sentence:** Mara discovers that Ellis can offer practical help without forcing a socially demanding exchange.

---

## 3. Start state

### Person A — Mara

**What does she want right now?**  
To get her grandmother's desk lamp repaired with as little live conversation as possible.

**What is she afraid of right now?**  
Being pulled into a long interaction, being asked why the lamp matters, or being visibly emotional in a public place.

**What does she think is happening?**  
A small practical errand that she wants to finish before her shift changes.

**What is she hiding, masking, or avoiding?**  
The lamp has emotional weight. It is not just an object.

### Person B — Ellis

**What does he want right now?**  
To understand what is wrong with the lamp and help without making Mara uncomfortable.

**What is he afraid of right now?**  
Over-talking, getting the interaction wrong, or seeming intrusive.

**What does he think is happening?**  
Mara has trusted him with something more important than the repair ticket says.

**What is he hiding, masking, or avoiding?**  
He has noticed for months that Mara avoids the loudest parts of the building, but he does not want to sound like he has been watching her.

---

## 4. Scene conditions

**Sensory load:** low to moderate. The repair area is quieter than the main hallway, but there is fluorescent hum, distant voices, and occasional trolley noise.

**Privacy / crowding:** semi-private. Other people can pass, but no one is directly listening.

**Time pressure:** Mara is between duties and wants the errand finished quickly.

**Routine disrupted or stable?:** mildly disrupted. Mara has inserted an extra errand into her workday.

**Person A condition:** guarded, controlled, slightly overloaded by the personal meaning of the lamp.

**Person B condition:** focused, careful, slightly anxious about doing too much.

---

## 5. Bond evidence

**What Person A notices about Person B:**  
Ellis writes down the repair estimate and collection time instead of making her wait through a spoken explanation.

**What Person B notices about Person A:**  
Mara relaxes slightly when the information is written down and when he does not ask why the lamp matters.

**What makes the bond more specific in this scene?**  
Their first connection is built through accurate adjustment, not charm. Ellis notices the form of communication that helps Mara, and Mara notices that he noticed without making a performance of it.

**Care action, if any:**  
Ellis offers a written collection time and a clear repair option. He also gives Mara a way to leave without social awkwardness.

---

## 6. Misread / accuracy map

**What Person A thinks is happening:**  
Ellis is unusually careful for a routine repair-desk interaction.

**What Person B thinks is happening:**  
Mara wants practical clarity and minimal speech.

**What is actually happening:**  
Both are creating the first safe communication pattern between them.

**What Person A gets right:**  
Ellis is being careful and he is adapting to her communication needs.

**What Person A gets wrong:**  
She assumes the interaction can stay emotionally small just because neither of them names the lamp's meaning.

**What Person B gets right:**  
Mara is more comfortable with written clarity than live over-explanation.

**What Person B gets wrong:**  
He underestimates how emotionally important the lamp is and how much trust the handover already requires.

---

## 7. Beat map

### Beat 1

**Action:** Mara arrives at the repair desk with the lamp wrapped in a plain cloth bag.  
**Meaning:** She is trying to make a personal object look like a simple errand.

### Beat 2

**Action:** Ellis asks one practical question, then waits instead of filling the silence.  
**Meaning:** He gives her processing space without announcing that he is doing it.

### Beat 3

**Action:** Mara gives the shortest possible explanation: flicker, loose switch, old plug.  
**Meaning:** She gives facts because facts feel safer than the object's history.

### Beat 4

**Action:** Ellis writes the repair estimate and collection time on a card.  
**Meaning:** He recognises the kind of clarity that helps her stay regulated.

### Beat 5

**Action:** Mara takes the card, nods, and leaves before the moment can become too personal.  
**Meaning:** The interaction is small, but it has created a first safe pattern.

---

## 8. Dialogue and subtext

**Most important spoken line:**  
"I can write the collection time down if that's easier."

**Why it matters:**  
It is the first moment Ellis offers care in a form Mara can actually use.

**What can be said directly:**  
The lamp needs repair. Mara wants a collection time. Ellis can fix it.

**What is still too hard to say directly:**  
The lamp matters because of Mara's grandmother. Ellis has noticed Mara's quiet patterns. Both are more affected by the interaction than they admit.

**What is shown through action instead of speech:**  
Ellis's restraint. Mara's relief. The beginning of trust.

---

## 9. Scene-end change

**What is now true that was not true before?**  
Mara has trusted Ellis with a meaningful object, and Ellis has learned that written clarity helps her feel safer.

**Relationship movement:** more clarity, first safe pattern, cautious trust

**Person A carries forward:**  
Ellis did not press. He noticed something useful and did not make her explain herself.

**Person B carries forward:**  
Mara's quietness is not emptiness. It may be a sign that the interaction matters.

---

## 10. Keep / revise / cut

**This scene must stay because:**  
It creates the first communication pattern and shows the romance's core logic in a practical, low-pressure moment.

**Decision:** keep

**Next action:** Draft the scene in Mara's point of view. Keep it quiet, concrete, and short. Do not over-explain the lamp's history yet.
