# What works best

## 1. The prose respects Mara's processing

The scene does not force her to explain herself. Her short answers and controlled attention feel functional, not broken.

## 2. Ellis is careful without being overpraised

The draft does not make him heroic. It shows one useful behaviour: he notices the form of help that fits the moment.

## 3. The lamp detail is enough

The brass base, green shade, and acorn pull chain give the lamp emotional texture without explaining the grandmother history too early.

## 4. The final line works

`No need to wait.` is a good ending phrase for this scene because it carries both practical meaning and emotional promise.

# Small revision needed

## Issue

The line below is slightly abstract:

```text
He had the careful look of someone who noticed loose hinges before conversation.
```

It is good, but it risks sounding author-shaped rather than fully Mara-shaped.

## Suggested fix

Replace with something more directly observed:

```text
He noticed loose hinges, flickering bulbs, chairs that scraped, labels peeling at the corners.
```

This keeps the same meaning but makes it more concrete.
