# Quiet Hours — Mini Design Sheet Test

## Purpose

This file tests whether the Mini Design Sheet is enough to start a romance project without opening the full Design Sheet first.

---

# 1. Project

**Working title:** Quiet Hours

**Romance type:** Quiet contemporary slow-burn romance.

---

# 2. Central pair

## Person A

**Name:** Mara

**Current life pattern:** Works in a community library. Keeps her day stable through routine, quiet rooms, written notes, and predictable tasks.

**What they want:** To be close to someone without being rushed into live emotional performance.

**What they fear in closeness:** Being watched, pressed, interpreted too quickly, or made responsible for someone else's anxiety.

## Person B

**Name:** Ellis

**Current life pattern:** Runs a repair desk in the same community building. Fixes objects well, but tries to fix emotional tension too quickly.

**What they want:** To be useful, wanted, and trusted.

**What they fear in closeness:** Silence, rejection, and the feeling that he has failed before he understands what happened.

---

# 3. Why these two?

1. Mara recognises that Ellis helps too much when he is anxious, not because he wants control.
2. Ellis recognises that Mara's silence means processing, not emptiness or rejection.
3. Both treat small practical acts as emotionally serious.

---

# 4. Emotional promise

**This romance promises the reader:** love can become safer when two people learn to tell the difference between silence, pressure, care, and fear.

---

# 5. Main obstacle

**Love is difficult because:** Mara experiences urgent help as pressure, while Ellis experiences silence as rejection.

---

**Main ND-readable truth in one sentence:** Regulation styles can look like rejection or control unless the characters learn to read each other more accurately.

---

# 7. Ending requirement

**The ending only works if:** Mara can ask for time without disappearing, and Ellis can offer care without trying to solve everything immediately.

---

# 8. First scene seed

**First scene idea:** Mara brings a broken desk lamp to Ellis during the building's quiet hour.

**Scene job:** First noticing.

**What changes by the end of the scene?** Mara trusts Ellis with the lamp, and Ellis realises written clarity helps her relax.

# Test verdict

The Mini Design Sheet works better as the first file than the full Design Sheet.

It captures the core romance quickly:

- pair
- specific bond
- emotional promise
- obstacle
- ND-readable truth
- ending requirement
- first scene seed

It is enough to move directly into the Structure Template.

## Remaining weakness

The Mini Design Sheet does not yet force a clear external pressure. That is acceptable for a first-pass file, but the Structure Template should catch it next.

## Recommendation

Keep the Mini Design Sheet as the default starting point in `FIRST_USE.md`.

Do not remove the full Design Sheet. Use it as the deeper second-pass file when the romance needs more development.
