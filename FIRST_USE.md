# First Use Workflow

Use this file when you want to start a romance project without reading the whole pack first.

## Good enough for the first pass

Do not try to perfect the book idea.

Good enough means:

- you know who the two people are
- you know why these two matter to each other
- you know what makes love difficult
- you know what kind of ending the story needs
- you have one rough structure path
- you have one blank scene card ready for the first scene

## Open these files first

1. `active_pack/ND_Readable_Romance_Design_Sheet_v1.0.md`
2. `active_pack/ND_Readable_Romance_Structure_Template_v1.0.md`
3. `active_pack/ND_Readable_Romance_Arc_Map_v1.0.md`
4. `active_pack/ND_Readable_Romance_Scene_Card_Template_v1.0.md`

Do not start with the checklist, triage sheet, or revision workflow.
Those are for checking and repair.

## First workflow

### Step 1 — Design Sheet

Fill only these parts:

- central pair
- why these two
- emotional promise
- main obstacle
- ND truth layer
- ending requirement

Stop when you can explain the romance in plain language.

### Step 2 — Structure Template

Choose a simple structure:

- opening contact
- deepening bond
- midpoint change
- pressure and fault line
- rupture or major test
- repair path
- ending proof

Do not fill every chapter yet.

### Step 3 — Arc Map

Write the relationship movement in rough stages:

- where they start
- what first changes
- what makes closeness risky
- what breaks or nearly breaks
- what repair requires
- what the ending proves

### Step 4 — Blank Scene Card

Use the template for one scene only.

Answer:

- what the scene job is
- what each person thinks is happening
- what is actually happening
- what changes by the end

## Files to avoid at first

Avoid these until you have a rough draft or a broken section:

- `active_pack/ND_Readable_Romance_Checklist_v1.0.md`
- `active_pack/ND_Readable_Romance_Draft_Failure_Triage_Sheet_v1.0.md`
- `active_pack/ND_Readable_Romance_Revision_Workflow_v1.1.md`

They are useful, but too heavy for starting.

## Stop rule

Stop the first session when you have:

- one simple romance definition
- one central obstacle
- one rough arc
- one planned scene

That is enough to begin.

## Next session

After the first session, open the Beat Tracker and map 5 to 10 rough beats.

Then draft or plan only the next scene.
