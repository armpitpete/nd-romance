# ND Readable Romance Scene Card Template v1.0

## Core use

Use one card per scene or one expanded card per chapter if the chapter is built around a single major movement.

This card is designed to stop:
- drift
- fake tension
- vague chemistry
- repeated emotional beats
- confusion mistaken for depth
- breakup logic that does not hold

This card is designed to strengthen:
- emotional clarity
- readable causality
- concrete care
- sensory truth
- trust progression
- earned romance structure

---

# Fast rule

A romance scene should usually do **one main job**.

If it is trying to do five things at once, split it.

A strong ND-readable romance scene usually contains:
- a clear emotional goal
- one main tension
- one meaningful interaction
- one visible shift by the end

# Romance-specific scene types

Use these mini-prompts when drafting particular scene functions.

---

## A. First meaningful encounter card

**Job:** make the connection feel specific, not generic.

Answer:
- Why does this meeting matter?
- What is unusual in their attention to each other?
- What is the first sign this person is not just “another person”?
- What detail stays with each of them after the scene?

Do not rely only on appearance.

---

## B. Care scene card

**Job:** turn attraction into evidence.

Answer:
- What need is noticed?
- How is care given without becoming preachy or sentimental?
- Why does the care land emotionally?
- What does the receiving character now realise?

Care is often more romantic than banter.

---

## C. Vulnerability scene card

**Job:** deepen attachment through real exposure.

Answer:
- What truth is risked?
- Why is saying it difficult?
- What is the listener expected to do with that truth?
- What would count as a good response?
- Does the response increase trust?

---

## D. Misattunement scene card

**Job:** create strain without fake stupidity.

Answer:
- What did each person actually mean?
- What did each person hear instead?
- Why did the misread happen?
- Why is it believable?
- What fault line does this expose?

A misattunement must reveal structure, not replace it.

---

## E. Rupture scene card

**Job:** break the relationship at the real fault line.

Answer:
- What has been building toward this?
- What finally becomes impossible?
- What fear response or failure causes the break?
- Why can this not be solved immediately?
- What does each person now believe?

If the breakup comes from nonsense, the whole romance weakens.

---

## F. Repair scene card

**Job:** show changed understanding through action and language.

Answer:
- What has been understood now that was not understood before?
- What is apologised for specifically?
- What behaviour changes?
- Why should the other person believe it?
- What remains difficult even after repair begins?

Repair should begin with accuracy, not spectacle.

---

## G. Union scene card

**Job:** make the final choice feel earned and durable.

Answer:
- What do they now know about each other?
- What real-life shape can this relationship take?
- What fear is still present but no longer ruling them?
- What promise is explicit?
- Why does the reader believe this will hold?

# Revision pass checklist

Use after drafting each scene.

## Keep
- [ ] The scene has one main emotional job.
- [ ] The point of view is stable and readable.
- [ ] The characters’ behaviour makes sense.
- [ ] Attraction is specific.
- [ ] The tension is real.
- [ ] The shift is visible by the end.
- [ ] Sensory reality supports the scene rather than clogging it.
- [ ] Care is shown concretely.
- [ ] Dialogue does not hide basic logic from the reader.
- [ ] The scene moves the romance spine forward.

## Cut or rewrite if true
- [ ] The scene repeats a previous beat without adding anything.
- [ ] The conflict is manufactured.
- [ ] The flirtation is generic.
- [ ] The scene depends on embarrassment rather than meaning.
- [ ] The emotional outcome is unclear.
- [ ] The characters say less than real people would for no reason.
- [ ] The scene could be removed and the romance would still work.

# Best practice notes

## Good ND-readable romance scenes often use:
- direct but not over-explained dialogue
- practical actions with emotional weight
- strong location clarity
- manageable cast size
- emotional transitions that can be named
- visible consequences

## Weak scenes often overuse:
- vague longing language
- repeated attraction beats
- social static
- irony instead of sincerity
- cruelty mistaken for tension
- unresolved mess with no structural purpose

---

# Recommended use order

1. Fill in the **structural job**
2. Fill in the **emotional function**
3. Fill in the **scene pressure**
4. Add **sensory and processing truth**
5. Build the **beat progression**
6. Draft dialogue
7. Check the **scene outcome**
8. Run the **revision pass checklist**

---

# One-sentence test

Before keeping any romance scene, ask:

**Does this scene make the relationship clearer, deeper, riskier, or more truthful?**

If not, it probably does not belong.
