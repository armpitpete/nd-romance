# ND Readable Romance Structure Template v1.0

## Core use

This file is the **structure-layer bridge** in the ND-readable romance pack.

Use it after:
- `ND_Readable_Romance_Design_Sheet_v1.0.md`

Use it before:
- `ND_Readable_Romance_Beat_Tracker_v1.0.md`
- `ND_Readable_Romance_Scene_Card_Template_v1.0.md`
- live chapter drafting

Its job is to stop:
- a good romance idea turning vague in the middle
- fake escalation
- rupture arriving too late or for weak reasons
- repair happening too quickly
- chapters that do not move the relationship
- structure being guessed scene by scene

This file defines:
- the manuscript spine
- the act or phase movement
- chapter-level relational jobs
- the midpoint shift
- the fault-line exposure
- the rupture logic
- the repair path
- the ending proof

---

# Core rule

A romance structure is working when the reader can track:

- why these two came into orbit
- what made the bond deepen
- what pressure made the bond unstable
- why the rupture happened
- why repair became possible
- why the ending is earned

If the structure cannot answer those clearly, the draft will blur later.

---

# One-line lock

**Every chapter must either build contact, deepen bond, sharpen strain, expose the fault line, force rupture, begin repair, or prove the ending.**

# What this file is not

This file is **not**:
- the concept-design file
- the line-by-line beat tracker
- the scene card
- the revision file
- a rigid formula for identical romance novels

This file **is**:
- the manuscript spine
- the chapter-job map
- the escalation control sheet
- the bridge between design and drafting

Do not fill this file until the design sheet can answer clearly:

- Why these two specifically?
- Why now?
- What kind of romance is this?
- What is the emotional promise?
- What is the main obstacle?
- What must change for union to become believable?

If those are not locked yet, go back to:
- `ND_Readable_Romance_Design_Sheet_v1.0.md`

# Section 4 — default structural guidance

Use this if you want a practical default and do not want to over-design.

## Default model: 18 chapters

### Chapters 1–4
**Job:** bring the leads into orbit and make contact matter

### Chapters 5–9
**Job:** deepen trust, attraction, care, and dependency

### Chapter 10
**Job:** midpoint shift; the relationship can no longer remain emotionally neutral

### Chapters 11–14
**Job:** pressure rises; the fault line becomes visible and costly

### Chapter 15
**Job:** rupture or relational break

### Chapters 16–17
**Job:** reflection, repair work, changed understanding, changed action

### Chapter 18
**Job:** ending proof; union or committed opening that is actually earned

Use a different chapter count if needed, but keep the same logic:
- opening contact
- deepening
- midpoint change
- pre-rupture strain
- rupture
- repair
- proof

# Section 9 — midpoint design test

The midpoint is often where a promising romance stops coasting and starts costing.

## Midpoint checklist

- [ ] Something becomes emotionally undeniable.
- [ ] The pair cannot honestly return to neutrality.
- [ ] The bond gains new weight.
- [ ] The story changes direction, not just intensity.
- [ ] A new risk becomes visible.

## Midpoint prompt

Complete all of these:

**Before the midpoint, they believed:**  
**At the midpoint, they realise:**  
**After the midpoint, they can no longer pretend:**  

## Midpoint failure warning

Revise if the midpoint is only:
- a kiss with no structural consequence
- a confession with no new pressure
- a plot event that does not alter the bond
- a rise in vibe with no rise in risk

---

# Section 10 — rupture design test

The rupture is not just “they stop talking.”  
It is the point where the established fault line becomes too costly to carry in the old way.

## Rupture checklist

- [ ] The rupture grows from earlier structure.
- [ ] The cause is emotionally specific.
- [ ] Both characters have understandable positions, even if one is more wrong.
- [ ] The rupture reveals a true fear, rule, or wound.
- [ ] The break changes the story state.
- [ ] The repair cannot be solved by one sentence.

## Rupture prompt

**What breaks:**  
**Why now:**  
**Why this hurts specifically:**  
**What false belief is exposed:**  
**What must change before repair is possible:**  

## Rupture failure warning

Revise if:
- the rupture depends on characters becoming briefly stupid
- the conflict could be solved in one normal sentence and the story knows that
- the break arrives before the bond is strong enough to matter
- the break happens too late for repair to feel earned
- the break is louder than the emotional logic supporting it

---

# Section 11 — repair path design test

A readable romance does not go from rupture straight to reunion without visible change.

## Repair checklist

- [ ] Someone re-reads what happened more truthfully.
- [ ] Someone changes behaviour, not just wording.
- [ ] The repair addresses the actual wound.
- [ ] The pair risk something new.
- [ ] The repair shows a new relational capacity.
- [ ] The ending proves the change under pressure.

## Repair prompt

**Who changes first in action:**  
**Who changes first in understanding:**  
**What apology is needed, if any:**  
**What proof is needed beyond apology:**  
**What new practice or agreement makes the ending believable:**  

## Repair failure warning

Revise if:
- the repair is only verbal
- one grand gesture replaces all structural work
- the more injured party accepts too quickly
- the story forgives without demonstrating safety
- the ending mood is warm but the relational problem is still alive

## Last-scene warning

The final chapter should not merely report happiness.
It should demonstrate changed capacity.

Examples:
- asking directly instead of performing
- staying present during overload or fear
- offering care without control
- naming need without shame
- choosing the relationship publicly after only being able to hold it privately
- reading silence accurately rather than catastrophically

# Section 13 — chapter pressure balance

A romance structure weakens when the same kind of scene repeats too often.

## Balance checklist

- [ ] not every chapter runs on near-identical attraction beats
- [ ] not every conflict beat is a miscommunication beat
- [ ] there is variation between privacy, pressure, relief, strain, and choice
- [ ] the bond is shown in action, not just sensation
- [ ] the middle contains clear relational movement
- [ ] the repair chapters have real work to do

## Variation prompts

- [ ] humour or ease
- [ ] desire

- [ ] choice under strain

# Section 16 — emergency simplification rule

If the structure feels too complicated, reduce it to this:

For every chapter, answer only:

1. Where do they stand at the start?
2. What happens that matters?
3. How does the bond or strain change?
4. Why can they not go back to the previous state?

If you cannot answer those, the chapter is not structurally ready.

# Section 17 — final lock

**A strong romance structure is a chain of clear relational changes.**

Not a pile of chemistry.
Not a pile of scenes.
Not a pile of almost-confessions.

The structure is working when each chapter makes the next chapter necessary.
