# ND Readable Romance Arc Map v1.0

## Purpose

Use this map to plan or revise a romance arc across chapters so that the relationship remains:

- emotionally legible
- structurally trackable
- readable without social fog
- clear in its progression, strain, and change

This is not a formula for sameness.  
It is a control tool for making sure the romance **moves clearly**.

---

# Core rule

A readable romance arc lets the reader track, chapter by chapter:

- where the relationship stands now
- what changed in this chapter
- what each person thinks is happening
- what is actually happening
- what new pressure now exists
- why the bond matters more than it did before

---

# One-line lock

**Each chapter must either deepen the bond, sharpen the strain, clarify the bond, or force the next relational choice.**

---

# Non-goals

This map does **not** require:

- rigid beat-sheet writing
- identical pacing in every book
- removal of subtext
- over-explaining every emotional shift
- flattening characters into “healthy communication only”

This map **does** require:

- visible movement
- clear turning points
- earned changes
- distinguishable states
- readable repair and escalation

---

# Pass condition

The romance arc is working if the reader can explain:

- how the bond formed
- how it changed
- what blocked it
- what each person learned or failed to learn
- why the ending is earned

without having to invent missing steps.

# Part 5: ND readability controls across the arc

## 1. Bond legibility

- [ ] The reader can say why the pair matters by the early-middle of the book.
- [ ] Attraction is specific, not generic.
- [ ] The bond is built from observable scenes, not just summary.

## 2. State distinction

Across the whole arc:

- [ ] overload does not read the same as rejection
- [ ] silence does not always read the same way
- [ ] care is distinguishable from pressure
- [ ] avoidance is distinguishable from indifference
- [ ] unmasking is distinguishable from collapse

## 3. Repair visibility

- [ ] misunderstandings are not all left floating
- [ ] repair happens on-page where it matters
- [ ] apology alone is not treated as enough
- [ ] changed behaviour appears after recognition

## 4. Pattern readability

- [ ] the arc includes recurring patterns the reader can track
- [ ] these patterns evolve, break, or deepen over time
- [ ] repetition builds meaning rather than stalling the book

## 5. Emotional cause and effect

- [ ] major turns have visible causes
- [ ] feelings shift because of scenes, not author wish
- [ ] the reader can point to the chapters that earn the ending

# Part 6: Arc stress test

Ask these before finalising.

## Plain-language arc test

Can I explain, in plain language:

- [ ] how they first mattered to each other?
- [ ] what made closeness difficult?
- [ ] what changed the bond?
- [ ] where they misread each other?
- [ ] what finally allowed truth, repair, or commitment?
- [ ] why the ending is earned?

If not, the arc is not yet clear enough.

## Gap test

- [ ] No major emotional leap happens without a bridging chapter or scene.
- [ ] Trust does not jump ahead without visible earning.
- [ ] Rupture does not happen without groundwork.
- [ ] Repair does not happen without changed understanding or action.

## Drift test

- [ ] Chapters do not keep repeating the same emotional beat.
- [ ] The arc does not stall in permanent almost-ness.
- [ ] The relationship changes in direction, pressure, or clarity across the book.

---

# Part 7: Red-flag failures

If these are true, revise the arc.

- [ ] the pair’s attraction is still vague halfway through
- [ ] the main conflict could be solved by one artificial conversation
- [ ] the arc relies on reader guesswork rather than visible progression
- [ ] one character exists mainly to interpret the other
- [ ] care has become burden-management
- [ ] the romance rewards manageability rather than deeper known-ness
- [ ] major turns are dramatic but not earned
- [ ] the ending resolves plot without resolving the relationship truth
- [ ] the reader would struggle to name the stages of the bond

---

# Part 8: Drafting use

Use while planning.

- [ ] I know the opening state of the bond.
- [ ] I know the key stages of progression.
- [ ] I know the main misread or strain pattern.
- [ ] I know the central turning point.
- [ ] I know what real repair would require.
- [ ] I know the ending state and how it differs from the beginning.

---

# Part 9: Revision use

Use after the draft exists.

## Structural revision pass

- [ ] mark the current stage of the romance in every chapter
- [ ] cut chapters with no relationship movement
- [ ] add bridges where emotional jumps are too large
- [ ] sharpen chapters where the bond is asserted but not shown
- [ ] clarify misreads that are currently too foggy
- [ ] strengthen the chapter(s) that earn the ending

## Final revision pass

- [ ] every major turn is supported by prior chapters
- [ ] every rupture has a readable cause
- [ ] every repair has readable labour
- [ ] the ending reflects the actual romance problem, not just plot closure

---

# Final lock

**The reader should be able to follow the romance as a sequence of readable changes, not as a blur of implied feeling.**

Depth is welcome.  
Fog is not.
