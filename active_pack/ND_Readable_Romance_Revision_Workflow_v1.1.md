# ND Readable Romance Revision Workflow v1.1

## Core use

Use this file to revise an ND-readable romance in the correct order.

Its job is to stop these mistakes:

- line-editing before the bond works
- polishing scenes that belong to a broken structure
- trying to fix a whole-book problem with local scene edits
- improving clarity before deciding what the story is actually trying to prove
- treating repair as a vague instinct instead of a sequence

This is the **canonical revision-order file** for the pack.

---

## One-line lock

**Do not line-edit a romance draft that is still failing at bond, structure, conflict, or readability level.**

---

1. `ND_Readable_Romance_Design_Sheet_v1.0.md`

7. `ND_Readable_Romance_Draft_Failure_Triage_Sheet_v1.0.md`

---

## Core rule

Always fix the **highest-level failure first**.

Normal order:

1. relationship engine and bond truth
2. structure and escalation
3. scene purpose and chapter movement
4. readability, state clarity, and ND truth
5. rupture, repair, and ending proof
6. sentence-level polish

Do not reverse this order unless the earlier layer is already solid.

---

## Entry routes

Choose the route that matches the manuscript.

- **Route A — new project, not yet drafted**
- **Route B — live draft in progress**
- **Route C — full draft complete but failing**
- **Route D — mostly working draft needing final proof**

---

# Route A — new project

Use this when the story is still at concept or structure stage.

## Step A1 — lock the romance engine

Use:

- `ND_Readable_Romance_Design_Sheet_v1.0.md`

### Job
Define what this romance is actually built on.

### Must be clear

- who the central pair are
- why these two matter to each other
- what emotional promise the book is making
- what the real obstacle is
- what the core fault line is
- what makes the story ND-readable rather than socially foggy
- what an earned ending would have to prove

### Do not move on if

- attraction is generic
- the obstacle is soft or fake
- the pair could be swapped with little change
- the ending requirement is still vague

---

## Step A2 — lock the structure spine

Use:

- `ND_Readable_Romance_Structure_Template_v1.0.md`
- `ND_Readable_Romance_Arc_Map_v1.0.md`

### Job
Map the book-wide movement of the relationship.

### Must be clear

- opening state
- progression stages
- midpoint pressure
- where the fault line is exposed
- where rupture happens
- what repair requires
- what the ending state proves

### Do not move on if

- the middle only repeats contact
- escalation is vague
- rupture is not prepared
- repair depends on hope rather than prior groundwork

---

## Step A3 — prepare scene control

Use:

- `ND_Readable_Romance_Scene_Card_v1.0.md`
- `ND_Readable_Romance_Beat_Tracker_v1.0.md`

### Job
Turn the structure into trackable chapter and scene movement.

### Must be clear

- what each chapter changes
- where the bond strengthens or strains
- where misread, care, distance, and repair fall
- how progression is being tracked

### Output
A draftable system, not just a premise.

---

# Route B — live draft in progress

Use this when chapters already exist but the manuscript is still forming.

## Step B1 — stop random drafting

Use:

- `ND_Readable_Romance_Beat_Tracker_v1.0.md`
- `ND_Readable_Romance_Scene_Card_v1.0.md`

### Job
Check whether the current draft still matches the intended movement.

### Ask

- what changed in the last chapter
- what must change next
- whether the bond is progressing or merely recurring
- whether the current scene has one readable job

---

## Step B2 — run a standards check

Use:

- `ND_Readable_Romance_Checklist_v1.0.md`

### Job
Catch drift before it hardens.

### Look for

- generic chemistry
- fake conflict
- repeated scene function
- point-of-view fog
- weak care evidence
- softness in the middle

---

## Step B3 — diagnose before rewriting

Use:

- `ND_Readable_Romance_Draft_Failure_Triage_Sheet_v1.0.md`

### Job
Name the failure precisely before you start editing.

### Typical failure types

- thin bond
- flat chemistry
- false conflict
- dead middle
- unearned rupture
- weak repair
- soft ending
- state-readability fog
- repetition without progression

---

# Route C — full draft complete but failing

Use this when the manuscript exists but is not working.

## Step C1 — identify the dominant failure

Use:

- `ND_Readable_Romance_Draft_Failure_Triage_Sheet_v1.0.md`

### Output
One dominant failure mode, plus any secondary failures.

Do not start with line edits.

---

## Step C2 — repair the engine first

Return to:

- `ND_Readable_Romance_Design_Sheet_v1.0.md`
- `ND_Readable_Romance_Structure_Template_v1.0.md`
- `ND_Readable_Romance_Arc_Map_v1.0.md`

### Use this when

- the pair do not feel necessary to each other
- the main conflict is fake
- rupture is unearned
- the ending lands weakly because the book never earned it

### Typical actions

- strengthen specific bond evidence
- replace fake delay with a real fault line
- rebuild escalation
- reseed the groundwork for rupture and repair

---

## Step C3 — repair chapter and scene movement

Use:

- `ND_Readable_Romance_Beat_Tracker_v1.0.md`
- `ND_Readable_Romance_Scene_Card_v1.0.md`

### Use this when

- chapters repeat the same emotional beat
- scenes are present but inert
- middle chapters do not change the relationship state
- scenes explain emotion instead of moving it

### Typical actions

- assign one primary job per chapter
- cut or merge duplicate beats
- increase chapter-to-chapter movement
- make emotional change visible on-page

---

## Step C4 — repair readability and ND truth

Use:

- `ND_Readable_Romance_Checklist_v1.0.md`

### Use this when

- state clarity is weak
- masking and legibility are underwritten
- overload, fear, shame, and withdrawal all read the same
- care is vague rather than specific
- characters are rewarded for better performance of normality rather than deeper recognition

### Typical actions

- sharpen internal state distinctions
- improve sensory and social condition cues
- make care precise
- show clearer legibility shifts between the pair

---

## Step C5 — repair ending proof

Return to:

- `ND_Readable_Romance_Arc_Map_v1.0.md`
- `ND_Readable_Romance_Checklist_v1.0.md`
- `ND_Readable_Romance_Beat_Tracker_v1.0.md`

### Ask

- what exactly does the ending prove
- what has each person changed, learned, or chosen
- whether repair is visible enough
- whether commitment feels earned rather than declared

---

# Route D — mostly working draft needing proof

Use this when the book largely works and now needs final tightening.

## Step D1 — run a final checklist pass

Use:

- `ND_Readable_Romance_Checklist_v1.0.md`

### Confirm

- the bond is specific
- the conflict is real
- the middle progresses
- the rupture is earned
- the repair is visible
- the ending proves the promise

---

## Step D2 — run a beat and scene compression pass

Use:

- `ND_Readable_Romance_Beat_Tracker_v1.0.md`
- `ND_Readable_Romance_Scene_Card_v1.0.md`

### Confirm

- no scene is redundant
- no chapter repeats the same relational job
- exposition is not doing the work of progression
- scene endings create actual state change

---

## Step D3 — only now do line-level polish

Do this only after the higher layers are stable.

### Focus

- clarity
- precision
- emotional legibility
- rhythm
- unnecessary explanation
- repeated sentence habits

# What not to do

Do not:

- rewrite the ending first
- line-edit a dead middle
- add banter to fake chemistry
- insert a bigger argument to fake stakes
- keep scenes that have no unique relational job
- treat the proof pass as a substitute for structural repair

---

# Closure rule

The draft is ready for final proof only when all of these are true:

- the bond is specific
- the conflict is real
- the middle progresses
- rupture is earned
- repair is visible
- the ending proves the story’s emotional promise
- remaining work is local, not structural
